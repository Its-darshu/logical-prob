# Day 1: The Farmer Problem (Wolf, Goat, and Cabbage)

## Problem Statement
A farmer has to cross a river with a wolf, a goat, and a cabbage. He has a small boat that can only carry himself and one other item at a time. The rules of nature apply: if left unattended, the wolf will eat the goat, and the goat will eat the cabbage. How can the farmer safely transport all items across the river?

## Rules / Constraints
* The boat holds only the farmer + 1 item (wolf, goat, or cabbage).
* Wolf + Goat alone = Wolf eats Goat (Failing State).
* Goat + Cabbage alone = Goat eats Cabbage (Failing State).
* The farmer can make as many trips as needed.

## Solution Steps
1 . farmer takes the goat first and leave it on other side 
2. framer goes backand bring the wolf and bring goat back to the original side
3. farmer leaves the goat and takes the cabbage to the other side
4. farmer goes back and bring the goat to the other side
